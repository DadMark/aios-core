## Step 1: Story Gathering

**elicit: true** -- This is a guided discovery. Ask each question and wait.

### WHY Discovery Questions

Ask these in sequence, going deeper with follow-ups:

1. **Origin story:** "Tell me about the moment this idea was born. What were you feeling?"
2. **Frustration:** "What about the status quo made you say 'there HAS to be a better way'?"
3. **Impact:** "Describe a time when your product/service genuinely changed someone's day. What happened?"
4. **Sacrifice:** "What have you given up to pursue this? Why was it worth it?"
5. **Legacy:** "If this brand succeeded beyond your wildest dreams, what would the world look like?"

## Step 3: WHY Validation

| Test | Question | Pass? |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Feel Test** | Does it make you feel something? (Limbic brain response) | |
| **Filter Test** | Can you use it as a decision filter? (Would you say no to opportunities that don't align?) | |
| **Tattoo Test** | Would you tattoo it on your arm? (Commitment level) | |

All 3 must pass. If any fail, return to Step 1 and dig deeper.

---

## Step 4: Brand Manifesto

Write the manifesto following this structure:

1. **Opening** -- The belief that drives everything
2. **The Problem** -- What's wrong with the status quo
3. **The Vision** -- What the world looks like when the WHY is realized
4. **The Invitation** -- How others can join the cause
5. **The Promise** -- What the brand commits to, always

**Rules:**
- Write in first person plural (WE believe...)
- Lead with emotion, support with logic
- Under 250 words
- Every sentence should make the reader feel something
- No corporate jargon -- write like a human being
